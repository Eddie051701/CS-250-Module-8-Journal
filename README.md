# CS-250-Module-8-Journal

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
An important part of interperating user needs is understanding that the reason the program exists is to solve the users problems.  If the program is perfect tecnically, but does not do what the user needs, it is then useless to the user.  Creating user stories provides direct feedback from the users with specific acceptance criteria, failure criteria, and an understanding of what users want.  This creates an understanding from developers on the needs of the users and gives them a definitive road to implimenting these changes.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach program development by breaking work down into managable peices, prioritizing the most important pieces first, and constantly testing and adapting the code I produce to fit new updates in requirements.  I will incorporate agile methods such as product backlogs, sprint planning, iterative development, and retrospectives to constantly create working code that evolves over time, and learn what works and what doesn't over time.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member in software development means bringing the proper skills and knowledge of a developer, but also supporting the success of the team by being reliable, accountable, and a great communicator.  Listening to teammates and helping them solve any issues they face, while also seeking out help when facing issues of your own creates an extremely efficien enviornment for problem-solving and teamwork.  These traits are crucial to contributing not just to the success of yourself, but the success of the whole team and delivery of a great product.
